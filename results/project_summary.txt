CINEMATCH PROJECT - FINAL SUMMARY
======================================

Date: Friday, 20 March 2026 02:15:00

PROJECT OVERVIEW
----------------
Semantic Movie Search using AI and Natural Language Processing.
CineMatch allows users to find movies by describing their plots, themes, or moods
in natural language rather than just searching by title or keywords.

DATASET
-------
Base: TMDB 5000 Movies (4,803 movies)
Enriched: TMDB + Wikipedia Plots
Wikipedia Match Rate: 74.5% (3,584/4,809)
Plot Length Improvement: +831.4% (305 to 2,843 characters)

MODELS TESTED
-------------
1. all-mpnet-base-v2
2. all-MiniLM-L6-v2
3. e5-small-v2

EVALUATION METRICS
------------------
Test Queries: 20
Datasets Compared: Base TMDB vs Enriched
Metrics: Precision@1, @3, @5, @10, and Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR)

BEST OVERALL CONFIGURATION:
  Dataset: Enriched
  Model: all-mpnet-base-v2
  MRR: 0.503 (Average Rank: 2.0)
  P@1: 40.0%
  P@3: 60.0%
  P@5: 65.0%
  P@10: 70.0%

KEY FINDINGS
------------
- Enriched dataset significantly outperformed base TMDB for the best model.
- Wikipedia plot enrichment improved P@5 accuracy by 10 percentage points (+18.1% relative).
- MRR improved from 0.445 to 0.503, meaning the correct movie appears earlier in results.
- Best configuration: all-mpnet-base-v2 + Enriched Dataset.
- Semantic search successfully matches natural language queries even when keywords are missing.
